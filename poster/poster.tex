\panel{25}{509}{350}{169}{Problem, Motivation, and Intake Gap}{
\textbf{Problem.} Patients describe symptoms in incomplete everyday language, while clinicians need structured history: onset, duration, severity, radiation, associated symptoms, risk factors, medications, allergies, and red flags.

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.34\linewidth}p{0.55\linewidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Gap} & \textbf{Medora response}\\
\midrule
Loose complaint & 15-field structured intake.\\
Missed red flags & Symptom-specific safety checks.\\
No traceability & CMDT chapter and section evidence.\\
Privacy risk & Hospital-server open-source runtime target.\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
}

\panel{25}{344}{350}{145}{Design Decisions}{
{\SmallFont
\begin{tabular}{p{0.30\linewidth}p{0.30\linewidth}p{0.31\linewidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Choice} & \textbf{Tried or considered} & \textbf{Final design}\\
\midrule
Knowledge & LLM memory, raw web, textbook & CMDT RAG plus governed web.\\
Chunking & Fixed windows & Structure-aware chunks.\\
Ranking & Bi-encoder only, MedCPT, BGE & BGE reranker.\\
Current evidence & Unrestricted search & PII sanitizer and source tiers.\\
Runtime & Hosted APIs & Local open-source target.\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
}

\vspace{2mm}
The Fall Healthify scope was narrowed into Medora so the project could show a deeper, testable clinical workflow rather than a broad wellness application.
}

\textbf{Area} & \textbf{Design response}\\

Safety & Red flags and emergency routing are surfaced early.\\

\bottomrule
